Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Rooftop solar panels are photovoltaic systems installed on the roof of a building to generate electricity from sunlight. These systems are used in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings to produce clean and renewable energy.

Rooftop solar panels capture sunlight and convert it into electricity using photovoltaic cells. The generated electricity can be used immediately, stored in batteries, or fed back into the grid through net metering systems.

Yes, rooftop solar panels can significantly reduce electricity bills and provide a high return on investment over time. Government incentives and net metering programs further enhance cost savings.

Rooftop solar panels have a lifespan of 25 to 30 years, with minimal maintenance required. Regular cleaning and occasional servicing ensure optimal performance over their lifetime.

Most rooftops can support solar panels, but factors such as roof material, angle, shading, and structural integrity should be considered. Our experts can assess your roof for compatibility and provide customized solutions.
